EXPLORATORY DATA ANALYSIS OF THE AFGHAN AND UKRAINIAN MIGRATION CRISES



Comparison in the Global News Media


In order to gain a better understanding of global attention towards the Afghan and Ukrainian Migration Crises, here's a side-by-side comparison of total appearances in global news media. The prominence of media attention demonstrates significance of these humanitarian events through space and time.

Notice how the peak of global news media attention for Afghanistan is at the onset of The Taliban's takeover in July/August 2021 and then levels off. Meanwhile, the peak of global news media attention for Ukraine is at the onset of the Russian invasion in February 2022 and still has not returned to pre-onset levels.

FTS Humanitarian Aid Allocations


Analysing data from the Financial Tracking System (FTS) , overseen by the United Nations Office for the Coordination of Humanitarian Affairs (OCHA), helps to better understand the global movement of humanitarian funds at the macro-level. FTS data is recorded between donors (government and private) and operational humanitarian actors (UN agencies, the Red Cross Movement, NGOs and CSOs).

Below are comparative bar charts that show funding/requirements by year. The Ukraine bar chart shows 3.7 bln funded/4.3 bln requested in 2022 and 814.6 mln funded/3.9 bln requested in 2023. The Afghanistan bar chart shows 1.5 bln funded/1.9 bln requested in 2021, 3.2 bln funded/4.4 bln requested in 2022, and 256.3 mln funded/4.6 bln requested in 2023.
