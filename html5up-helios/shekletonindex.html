Hi! You're looking at Power Dynamics of Global Philanthropy.

My name is Caitlin Ann Shekleton and I'm the creator of this Capstone Project.

Curated over a period of eight months' worth of independent research, this project is the culmination of my B.A. in Global Studies (Middle East/South Asia) at the University of Virginia.

With the underlying assumption that there is a "hiearachy of humanity, inequality of lives" present in the global philanthropy ecosystem, this project seeks to understand the global movement of charitable funds towards the Afghan and Ukrainian Migration Crises. Written research and accompanying data visualisation quantifies philanthropic behaviour in the Global North versus Global South. Particular focus is on digital innovation in the form of the online crowdfunding platform. This mode of transnational digital-social connection is pushing back against asymmetrical power dynamics in the global philanthropy ecosystem, creating potential to shift a greater sense of agency to the subaltern voice via diaspora philanthropy.

Project Ideation

Here's content that captures some of the values, themes, and key ideas which inspired this project.

WAR IS HELL

Creative artist @collagovka conveys traditional Ukrainian artistry through digital medium. This piece symbolises both the hope and resistance of the Ukrainian people... as well as the inhumanity of war.

WOMEN'S RIGHTS

This artwork by Afghani artist Sara Rahmani depicts, "The sad situation of closing schools for girls, the struggle of women and girls to regain their rights, and the attempts of people to escape Afghanistan."

EMPATHY

A visual diary of black & white photographs taken by Peter Turnley of Ukrainian refugees in March 2022. Thru the lens of the camera, "the heartbreak and inspiring moments of people in flight" are captured.

HUMANITARIAN RACISM

Trevor Noah highlights the existence of humanitarian racism, which has surfaced as a double-standard in refugee treatment especially in wake of the Ukrainian War. Universal acceptance of refugees exists in theory.

WELCOME ALL REFUGEES

"Creating a stronger culture of welcome is the only way to replace narratives and policies of cruelty and indifference." The human right to mobility transcends race, gender, religion, and politics.

Journal Article

For a written articulation of my research and findings

ABSTRACT: The digital age is challenging conventional methods of raising humanitarian funds, especially with the recent emergence of online crowdfunding platforms. Perhaps the most notable element of online crowdfunding is that it holds potential for the empowerment of diaspora communities to steer the distribution of global philanthropic funds towards localised causes, which are often left on the periphery of macro-scale global humanitarian responses. Using a contemporary case study of the Afghan and Ukrainian Migration Crises, this research deploys data science and digital ethnography methods to cross-compare the online crowdfunding response. First, a literature review provides a baseline understanding of online crowdfunding and diaspora philanthropy, and its role in the Afghan and Ukrainian Migration Crises, thereby unpacking the hidden power dynamics between the Global North and Global South. Philanthropy data from two popular crowdfunding platforms, GoFundMe and Global Giving, is then transformed into a series of data visualisations in an attempt to quantify the overall impact of online crowdfunding. Finally, digital ethnography seeks to analyse and humanise the sentiment of diaspora communities who participate in online crowdfunding. Overall research findings provide both critique and insight about the realities of asymmetrical power dynamics within the global philanthropic ecosystem - as well as offers an optimistic outlook for online crowdfunding as an untapped solution for creating a more equitable philanthropic landscape. Greater investment in the scaling and infrastructural development of online crowdfunding platforms, which display high levels of attracting diaspora philanthropy, can help to bridge-the-gaps in funding for global humanitarian crises and provide agency to subaltern voices of the Global South.

Key Words: Global South, Power, Data Visualisation, Crowdfunding, Diaspora Philanthropy
